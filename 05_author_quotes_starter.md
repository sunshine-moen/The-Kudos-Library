# Author Quotes Starter Set — The Kudos Library

**Purpose:** seed content for the `author_quote` table. Quotes appear in the footer of every system email. New quote each send (best-effort dedupe — same recipient doesn't see the same quote in their last 5 sends).

**Editorial criteria:**
- All authors must meet the "squeaky clean legacy" bar set by the badge list (see PRD §14).
- Quotes lean toward themes that fit a workplace celebration: kindness, attention, community, sustained practice, beauty in small moments, generosity, storytelling, gratitude. **No political quotes, no quotes about suffering or oppression in a heavy way.**
- Length cap: 300 characters (Resend HTML email comfortably handles this; fair-use comfort zone).
- Each quote attributed to author + source work where known.
- Canadian-author-weighted, matching badge representation: Indigenous, Black, Sri Lankan-Canadian, white Canadian; plus internationally beloved voices for variety.

---

## ⚠️ Verification note (please read before seeding production)

This list was assembled from my training data. Quote misattribution is a real risk, and the Thomas King badge episode is fresh evidence that "I'm sure I've seen this quote attributed to X" isn't good enough.

**Before seeding `author_quote` for production, please verify each entry against a primary source:**
- ✓ marked entries — high confidence (very widely circulated; very famous quote)
- ~ marked entries — medium confidence (commonly attributed; verify exact wording and that the author actually said/wrote it)
- ⚠ marked entries — lower confidence (verify especially carefully)

Recommended verification sources:
- The author's actual books (most reliable)
- Goodreads (cross-reference attributed source; flag if unsourced)
- The Canadian Encyclopedia, Writers' Trust of Canada (for Canadian authors)
- Author's official site or estate-authorized collections

**Cut anything you cannot verify.** A smaller curated set of 15 verified quotes is better than 30 with one misattribution that erodes trust.
